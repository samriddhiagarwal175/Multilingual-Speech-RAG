She was very fond of the young ladies whom she had known when Jane was their mistress here and Beth was her special favorite. The housekeeper let the way in Beth followed, for a moment Beth stood staring while the new maid regarded her with composure and a slight smile upon her beautiful face. She was dressed in the regulation costume of the maid's at Elmhurst, a plain black gown with a white apron and cap, then she gave a little laugh and replied, no, Miss Beth, I'm Elizabeth the Parsons. But it can't be, protested the girl, I attend to the household bending, you know, and care for the linen. You speak like an educated person, said Beth, wonderingly, where is your home? For the first time, the maid seemed a little confused, and regains wandered from the face of her visitor. She sat down in a rocking chair, and classping her hands in her lap, rocks slowly back and forth. I'm sorry, said Beth.
