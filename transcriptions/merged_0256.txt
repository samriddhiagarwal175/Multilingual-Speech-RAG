To the young American, here or elsewhere, the paths to fortune are innumerable, and all open. There is invitation in the air and success in all his wide horizon. He has no traditions to bind him or guide him, and his impulse is to break away from the occupation his father has followed, and make a new way for himself. The modus fellow would have liked fame thrust upon him for some worthy achievement. It might be for a book, or for the skillful management of some great newspaper, or for some daring expedition like that of Lieutenant strain or Dr. Cane. He was unable to decide exactly what it should be. Sometimes, he thought he would like to stand and a conspicuous pulpit, and humbly preach the gospel of repentance, And it even crosses mind that it would be noble to give himself to a missionary life to some benigned region where the date palm grows and the nighten gills voice is in tune and the bulbol sings on the off nights.
