Disdainful of a success of which Medam showed no acknowledgment, he thought of nothing, but boldly regaining the market preference of the princess. By degrees all his happiness, all his brillency, subsided into regret and uneasiness, so that his limbs lost their power, his arms hung heavily by his sides, and his head drooped as though he was stupified. King, who had from this moment become in reality the principal dancer in the quadril, cast a look upon his vanquished rival. Everyone could observe his agitation and frustration, a frustration which was, indeed, the more remarkable since people were not accustomed to see him with his arms hanging listlessly by his side. His head bewildered, and his eyes, with all their bright intelligence, be dimmed.
