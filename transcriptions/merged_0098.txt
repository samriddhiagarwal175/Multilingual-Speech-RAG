I tell him to give me some coffee if it is good. Then turning towards me, he says that I look like a foreigner, and when I say that I am an Italian, he begins to speak to me of the court, the city, of the theatres, and at last he offers to accompany me everywhere. I thank him, and take my leave. I address him in Italian, and he answers very wittily, but his way of speaking makes me smile and I tell him why. My remark pleases him, but I soon proved to him that it is not the right way to speak. However, perfits may have been the language of that ancient writer. I see a crowd in one corner of the garden. Everybody standing still on looking up. Is there not a meridian everywhere? Yes, but the meridian of the Palais Royale is the most exact. That is true, bad-delt-gray. All these honest persons are waiting their turn to get their snuff boxes filled.
